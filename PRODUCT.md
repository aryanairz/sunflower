# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Families and young people in Edinburg and the wider Rio Grande Valley planning a celebration: quinceaneras, gender reveals, birthdays, graduations, baby showers, private parties. They mostly arrive on a phone from Instagram or TikTok, scrolling fast, deciding in seconds whether this place feels right for their event. The decision is emotional first (does it look like a good time, is it pretty, can I picture my party here) and practical second (date, capacity, how to book). Many are bilingual; the tone is warm and personal, not corporate.

## Product Purpose

The marketing site for Sunflower Ranch, a family-owned outdoor event venue. It exists to make a visitor feel the warmth of the place and then move them into one action: sending an inquiry with their date. Success is an inquiry submitted or a phone call placed. The site is not a booking engine and not a brochure; it is the first handshake with a family business.

## Brand Personality

Warm, handcrafted, celebratory. Family-run, not franchised. It should feel like a hand-painted ranch sign at golden hour, like a backyard party your favorite tia throws, like Texas Hill Country hospitality with color and music. Voice: plain, friendly, a little playful, proud of being local. Three words: sun-baked, generous, joyful.

## Anti-references

- Clinical or wellness-spa minimalism (its own origin as a duplicated IV-therapy site; scrub every trace of that calm-clinical feeling).
- Generic SaaS marketing: cool grays, centered icon-title-text card grids, hero-metric blocks.
- The editorial-magazine reflex: Fraunces or Playfair display serif, tiny uppercase tracked labels stacked above every section heading, broadsheet rules, monochrome restraint. This brand is warm and colored, not a literary quarterly.
- Wedding-industrial-complex formality: cold marble, sterile script-on-white, stuffy gold-foil luxury. This is a fun family ranch, not a five-star ballroom.

## Design Principles

- Handmade warmth over corporate polish. Texture, hand-drawn marks, and imperfect edges beat slick uniformity. It should look made by people, for people.
- Let the place do the talking. The photography is the hero; chrome stays out of its way. One decisive photo beats a wall of them.
- Joyful, not precious. Celebratory energy and color. Approachable like a quinceanera, never stuffy.
- Local and rooted. Speak plainly, name Edinburg and the RGV, sound like a family that lives down the road.
- One clear next step. Every page funnels to the inquiry. Booking is a conversation you start, not a transaction you complete.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Warm gold reads only as an accent or at large sizes against cream; body text uses the espresso ink for contrast. Honor prefers-reduced-motion (entrance reveals, photo tilts, and the spinning sunflower all settle to static). Gallery lightbox, mobile menu, and the inquiry form are fully keyboard operable with visible focus. Alt text is written in the brand voice and carries real meaning. Touch targets are generous for one-handed phone use.
